MODULE 1: Pre-Midterm Foundations

Math foundations, core methods, and line search. Everything covered before the midterm. If you never took multivariable calculus, Page 02 is your lifeline.

 06Gradient Descent Deep Dive
Blindfolded mountain descent analogy. The full algorithm with every line explained. Exact step size derivation on quadratics. Why condition number controls convergence (bathtub analogy). SVG contour plots showing zigzag vs direct paths. Mirrors Assignment 1.
 07Newton's Method for Optimization

MODULE 2: Post-Midterm Core Methods

These are the final exam topics. Each page mirrors the exact numerical setup from your quizzes and assignments. Deep explanations, extensive diagrams, multiple worked examples.

09Trust-Region Methods
Safety zone analogy. Ratio test = weather forecaster's track record. Cauchy point. Algorithm 4.1 (color-coded). Theorem 4.1 optimality conditions. SVG: trust circle on contour plot. Hand computation mirrors exact Quiz 4 setup.
 10Conjugate Gradient Methods
Smart hiker analogy (never revisits directions). A-conjugacy vs orthogonality (deep explanation). Linear CG algorithm step-by-step. Why CG solves n-dim problems in n steps. Hand computation mirrors exact Quiz 5 setup. Full 2-iteration convergence.
 11Nonlinear Conjugate Gradient
Extending CG from linear to nonlinear. Fletcher-Reeves formula and algorithm. Polak-Ribière and PR+ variants. Automatic restart property. SVG: CG path vs GD path on elongated contours. When and why to use nonlinear CG.
 12BFGS Method
Learn-curvature-from-gradient-history analogy. Secant condition (deep explanation). BFGS update: both direct and inverse forms with full symbol breakdown. Curvature condition and Wolfe connection. Hand computation mirrors exact Quiz 6 setup. Assignment 2 Q1 proof.
 13SR1 and DFP Methods
Rank-1 vs rank-2 update comparison. SR1 formula with full derivation. Safeguard condition (why and when). DFP as dual of BFGS. Comparison table: BFGS vs SR1 vs DFP. Hand computation mirrors exact Assignment 2 Q2 setup.
 14Newton's Method for Nonlinear Equations
Tangent-line root-finding analogy. Scalar Newton (finding √2). Newton for vector systems with Jacobian. Inexact Newton. Broyden's method (BFGS for equations). Hand computation mirrors exact Quiz 7 setup.
 15Constrained Optimization & KKT Conditions
Fenced yard optimization analogy. Lagrangian with "price" interpretation. All 4 KKT conditions: stationarity, primal feasibility, dual feasibility, complementary slackness; each with its own analogy. LICQ. Hand computation mirrors exact Quiz 8 setup.
 16Linear Programming & Interior Point Methods
Tilted surface in a box analogy. Corner point optimality. Barrier = repulsive force field near walls. Central path. KKT for LP. SVG: feasible region with objective gradient. Connection back to Quiz 8.
